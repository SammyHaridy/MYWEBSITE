BACKGROUND INFO

My name is Sammy Haridy. I'm enrolled in Brooklyn Technical High School in the finance major, and on the side, I'm privileged to attend the All Star Code Summer Intensive. I've come to learn much so far, but I know I've barely scratched the surface. I'm still learning the basics, so I'm not yet proficient in any language as of now. This website marks my first debut in my lengthy career of computer science.

MY BACKSTORY

Starting off in Brooklyn, I attended an Islamic school for elementary and middle school. My father is Egyptian, my mother Moroccan. I always preferred my Moroccan side, as I was closer to them. As a child, my career interests never remained the same, but one of them was engineering. It took me until high school to stumble upon computer science. At first it was the money, but the experience of coding trumped my greed.

CULTURAL BACKGROUND

I'm half Egyptian, half Moroccan. I've been to Egypt only once when I was a child, so I don't remember much from it. But I do speak the dialect somwehat, and I make and enjoy the food. Sometimes my father makes Egyptian food at home. I've been more exposed, however, to my Moroccan side. Ever since I was a child, it was mint tea every day, and some sort of ethnic dish every afternoon. I spoke Darija all the time, and now I'm learning french.

It helped to read, as it enhanced my thinking and my creativity. As a result, I've grown somewhat and placed my struggle behind me.
